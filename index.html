❓ CC Troubleshooting & FAQ

When reaching out to creators, identify the key factors that best align with campaign objectives:

  • Content fit: Check thumbnails (avoid overly sensual, exaggerated, inappropriate). Ensure overall content aligns with brand positioning.
  • Size of creator: Micro (~50K), Medium (50K - 200K), Macro (200K - 500K), Magetron (>500K).
  • Views: Use Average Views if stable. Use Median Views if fluctuating.
  • Past works: Ensure they haven't worked with direct competitors within a 3-month timeframe.
  • Number & content of comments: Identical view counts across videos or high views with low comments indicate bots.
  • Upload Frequency: Rule out creators whose last video is >3 months old.

Identify the average/median views and the Client's CPM.
Example: Average views = 50K | Client’s CPM = $25. Ideal Price: 50 x $25 = $1,250

  • Present the creator to the client with the raw rate plus our margin, allowing the client to decide.
  • Negotiate first based on expected budget. Communicate clearly that this is a target price, not a confirmed offer.
  • Dupe checker failure: Ownership goes to whoever reached out first.
  • Not in dupe checker: If CC(A) has a conversation going, they keep it. If no response, CC(A) pauses and transfers to CC(B).
  • Long-term collaboration: CC(A) with an ongoing relationship should handle it.

Always acquire an ‘Unlisted link’ prior to posting. If a creator fails to complete deliverables, request makeup content. If they refuse, suggest raising the creator price to the client to make up for the mistake.

Chapter X: The Trial Campaign & Final Conversion Protocol

When a Campaign Coordinator (CC) receives an "Approved" rating, they enter a 1-to-2-month "Trial Phase" to prove their practical abilities by managing a mid-to-small-scale campaign. Only after completing Round 1 delivery and passing the final Debriefing session will they be officially granted the Campaign Executive (CE) title.

Step 1: Initiating the Trial Campaign

  • Title Transition: Internal title becomes Acting CE.
  • Assignment: CM assigns a manageable campaign (e.g., 3-5 creators).
  • The Shadow Manager: A CM/Senior CE is CC'd on all emails but remains silent. They only step in if the Acting CE is about to make a critical, irreversible mistake.

Step 2: Execution & Round 1 Completion

  • Client Kick-off: Personally hosts the meeting and clarifies details.
  • Day 5 Health Check: Independently reviews the funnel and triggers pivots.
  • Margin Control: Independently utilizes the Pricing Calculator for negotiations.
  • Round 1 Delivery: Successfully pushes the first batch of creators to "Go Live".

Step 3: The First-Round Debriefing

A 1-on-1 Debriefing with the CM after Round 1 goes live to review:

  • Data & Timeline Review: Did the actual margin stay above 25%-30%?
  • Scenario Breakdown: Walk through the thought process of 1-2 crises that occurred.
  • Team Health Check: Did they act as a "Translator/Shield" or a micromanager?
  • Self-Reflection: Share one win and one area for improvement.

Step 4: Official Conversion

✅ Pass: Officially promoted to Campaign Executive.
🔄 Extend Trial: If significant flaws are exposed, execute Round 2 to improve weak points.

⏳ Client Delay in Response Protocols

⚠️ The Universal Escalation Rule (3-5-5+)

Never wait until the absolute deadline to follow up. If an approval is blocking the workflow, use this timeline:

3 Days Late

The Gentle Nudge

CE replies to the existing email thread: "Hi [Name], bumping this to the top of your inbox..."

5 Days Late

Consequence Warning

CE clearly states the impact (e.g., losing creators, delaying Go-Live). Uses Assumptive Close if possible.

More than 1 week Late

CM Escalation

CM steps in for an "Agency Level" intervention to protect the contract and margin.

Stage 1

Creator Shortlist Review

Client ghosts after the CE presents the curated list of creators.

🔥 The Risk

Top-tier creators rarely hold integration slots for more than 48-72 hours. Delaying here means starting Phase 1 (Sourcing) all over again, killing the agency's internal margin.

🛡️ CE Action Plan

  • Warn the client about creator inventory limits politely.
  • Emphasize that a high turnaround time is necessary to secure the best creators for their campaign.
Warning Script
Hi [Client],

Hope you are doing well. I just want to check in on the creator list! Please let me know what do you think about the creator list presented. Do note that highly requested creators typically only hold their integration slots for 48 hours without a signed contract, while the remaining creators should be able to wait for a certain period of time, but it would still be great to keep a high turnaround time to better carry out the campaign.

Looking forward to hearing from you soon.
Stage 2

Script & Concept Review

Client ghosts while the creator is waiting for approval to start filming.

🔥 The Risk

The creator misses their shooting schedule. They might push the integration to a later video (delaying the campaign by weeks) or drop out entirely.

🛡️ CE Action Plan

  • Firmly but politely tie the approval delay to the final "Go Live" date.
  • Remind the client of the direct correlation between their review speed and the project timeline.
Warning Script
Hi [Client],

Just bubbling this up! [Creator Name] is scheduled to shoot their video tomorrow.

It will be great if we can provide them with final script approval by [Date/Time], or we might have to push their Go-Live date back by [X] days.

Please let me know if you have any edits as soon as possible

Thanks
Stage 3 (Critical)

The delay of the Creator list/Script or Video Draft Review

Client ghosts for more than a week in each phase of the campaign, which jeopardizing the timeline of the campaign.

🔥 The Risk

Long turnaround time results in cause time consumption, and time equals money, which is to say that the delay in the campaign timeline usually costs us a fortune. In this case, report to your CM just so they can jump in.

🚨 CM Escalation Trigger

  • If the client misses the review of each phase and ghosts CE's message for more than a week, escalate to the CM immediately.
  • CM will jump in and take over the conversation before the client gets back on track.
CM Intervention Script
Hi [Client],

I am stepping in from the management side regarding [Creator Name]'s video/script draft.

As per our standard creator agreements, feedback must be provided within 72 hours to avoid delaying the production and the publication window.

It will be great if we can hear back from you within 48 hours, or else the timeline might be pushed further. Thank you for your understanding, and we look forward to your timely response.

Thanks
  1. Identify Red Flags: Scope creep, unresponsiveness, legal risk, supply issues. Document factually.
  2. Attempt Remediation: Revise contract, suggest alternative creators/niches, adjust KPIs.
  3. Evaluate Cost vs Benefit: Is the client profitable? Is there reputation risk?
  4. Internal Approval: Present case to management and get written approval to terminate.
  5. Plan Exit Strategy: Decide timeline, pause campaigns cleanly, prepare final invoices.
  6. Communicate Professionally: "After careful review, we believe we are not the best fit..."
  7. Document Everything: Keep records of all emails, calls, and contract terms.
  8. Internal Debrief: Analyze what went wrong to refine future vetting.
Template 1: The Pivot vs. Clean Break (Market Reality)
Subject: Campaign Update & Strategic Review: [Project Name]

Dear [Client Name],

I’m writing to give you a transparent update on the current status of the [Campaign Name] creator outreach. Despite our team's consistent follow-ups, we are seeing an unusually low response rate ([X]% compared to our agency average of [Y]%). Among the few who replied, the feedback has been that they are currently pausing all financial service promotions due to audience sensitivity.

We believe in being 100% honest. At this stage, forcing the outreach further is unlikely to yield the high-quality results we promised you. We propose two options:
1. Strategic Pivot: We pause the direct acquisition angle and try a test group of educational creators.
2. Campaign Closure: We close out the current contract, provide you with our outreach data/market research, and refund the remaining unspent budget.

Could we hop on a quick 10-minute call this Thursday to decide which path you prefer?
Template 2: The Final Clean Break
Subject: Campaign Closure & Final Settlement: [Campaign Name]

Dear [Client Name],

Following our recent discussions regarding the structural challenges in the [Region] market for the [Vertical], we have decided to formally close the current outreach campaign effective [Date].

Rather than continuing to spend resources against a "closed door," we believe the most professional move is to stop here and preserve your remaining budget.

Next Steps & Deliverables:
1. Market Intelligence Report: We will hand over our full outreach database. This serves as a comprehensive feasibility study.
2. Financial Settlement: Total Budget utilized was $[Amount]. We have initiated a refund of $[Amount] for the unspent creator budget.

We still strongly believe in your team. Thank you for trusting us with this market test.

🚨 Crisis Triggers

Notify Campaign Manager immediately if:

  • 🚩 48 hours from a deadline, and the client is ghosting.
  • 🚩 Client rejects 80% of initial shortlist.
  • 🚩 Client ghosts for more than a week.

❓ CE Troubleshooting & FAQ

Google search the related topic, and collect more information online regarding the issue that was raised. After that, send a complete report (PDF) to the client and ask the client about the reason behind it. If it’s too fishy, please consult your CM.

When, after the 3rd list is presented, but the client still doesn’t select many creators, nor provide the proper reason for denial, in order to not waste too much of our time, move the campaign to the passive and offboarding phase. Please refer to the Offboarding Client SOP.

  • Budget & Pricing: Exact total budget cap and Target CPM (e.g., $20,000 for Q1 with $20 CPM).
  • KPIs & Goals: Primary metric (Brand Awareness, App Downloads, Lead Gen).
  • Target Niche: Creator categories, forbidden topics, brand-safety guidelines.
  • Deliverables: Platform, format (e.g., 60-90s integration), usage rights.
  • Timeline: Absolute "Go Live" date to reverse-engineer internal deadlines.
  • Creative Brief & CTA: Key talking points, tracking links, Call to Action.

This is a critical margin error. Response depends on timing:

  • Immediate Catch: Send a polite correction email. “Hi [Client], please excuse the typo in my previous email. The correct total investment is $X.”
  • If Approved: You must usually honor the price to maintain trust (taking 0% margin). The CE must strictly use the Pricing Calculator to optimize margins on the remaining creators to offset this loss.
  • Prevention: Enforce the "Pricing Gate" SOP—never send prices without running them through the Pricing Calculator first.

Use the "Consequence-Based Escalation" tactic:

  • Gentle Nudge: Reply highlighting urgency.
  • The "Loss" Warning: Frame delay as a loss. "Top-tier creators only hold slots for 48 hours. If we cannot get approval by [Date], we will lose these candidates."
  • The Assumptive Close: "If we don't hear from you by EOD Wednesday, we will proceed with Option A to keep the timeline on track."

This triggers the Crisis Trigger: 80% Shortlist Rejection. Do NOT tell the CC to "blindly find more."

  • Stop & Align: Pause sourcing. Get client on a 10-min call to identify the exact reason (Aesthetic? Metrics?).
  • Calibrate Filter: Update the "Off-Brand Filter" in the Internal Brief.
  • Trigger "Round 2": Go to Day 5 backups. Since you followed the 3:1 Sourcing Rule, you should have backups ready.
  • Use Data: "We reached out to 50 creators, response rate is 2%. Market feedback shows this niche is oversaturated."
  • Use Script: Send Template 1 (Pivot vs. Clean Break). Offer a Strategic Pivot (expand niche) or Campaign Closure (stop forcing budget, refund unspent budget to build trust).
